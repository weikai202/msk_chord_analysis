Racial and Ethnic Disparities in Driver Gene Mutations in Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer

Leveraging MSK-CHORD 2024 (n = 25,040) to examine whether driver gene mutation frequencies differ by race/ethnicity and how those differences associate with overall survival after multivariable adjustment.

Key Findings

Three robust findings replicated after multivariable adjustment.

EGFR enrichment in Asian patients — adj. OR 2.93 (95% CI 2.43–3.53), FDR < 0.001 Asian patients carry a 54.0% EGFR mutation rate vs. 20.2% in White patients. This association survives full covariate adjustment and is specific to NSCLC — not replicated in CRC or Breast cohorts, pointing to lung-tissue-specific biology or population-level germline variation.
KRAS and STK11 deficit in Asian patients — KRAS adj. OR 0.47; STK11 adj. OR 0.38 White patients have the highest smoking rate (78.8%) and correspondingly the highest KRAS (31.2%) and STK11 (14.5%) mutation rates. Both deficits in Asian patients survive adjustment, suggesting smoking-mediated and biology-mediated components coexist.
EGFR mutation consistently improves survival within every racial group EGFR-mutant patients gain 11–20 months of median OS vs. wild-type counterparts across all groups. The Cox Race × EGFR interaction term is not statistically significant after full adjustment, suggesting the benefit operates through treatment access (TKI availability) rather than differential tumour biology.
Black patients: youngest (median 67 yr), highest Stage IV rate (50.8%), shortest OS (18.7 mo) After adjusting for stage and smoking, Black patients' survival disadvantage attenuates but persists, consistent with residual confounding by socioeconomic factors or unmeasured treatment access barriers.

Cross-cohort comparison of adjusted ORs for all eight genes. Side-by-side comparison of ORs from complete-case analysis (blue) and MICE multiple imputation (m = 20, red) pooled by Rubin's rules. Consistent point estimates and overlapping CIs confirm that the complete-case results are not materially biased by the ~28% smoking-history missingness.

Statistical Methods

Six-step pipeline implemented in R with full reproducibility. Master script: Rscript R/07_run_all.R.

Step 1 Data Processing

Downloaded via cbioportalR. Race harmonised to OMB classification; critical fix prevents "Non-Spanish; Non-Hispanic" from matching the Hispanic substring. MICE imputation (m = 20, maxit = 10) for sensitivity.

Step 2 Descriptive Statistics

Table 1 via gtsummary. Mutation prevalence with Wilson CIs (preferred over Wald CIs near p = 0 or 1). Group differences by Kruskal-Wallis and chi-square.

Step 3 Association Tests

Chi-square when all expected counts ≥ 5; Fisher's exact (B = 10,000) otherwise. BH-FDR at q = 0.05 across 8 simultaneous tests. Effect size: Cramér's V.

Step 4 Logistic Regression

Binary outcome per gene. Histology collapsed to 3 categories to avoid complete separation. Falls back to Firth penalised regression on convergence failure. EPV ≥ 10 checked before fitting.

Step 5 Survival Analysis

Kaplan-Meier + log-rank. Cox PH with RACE_ETH × MUT_EGFR interaction. PH assumption tested via Schoenfeld residuals (cox.zph()). Tied times: Efron's method.

Step 6 Replication

Same logistic pipeline applied to CRC and Breast cohorts. Three-cohort forest plot visualises tissue-specificity vs. pan-cancer racial genomic effects.
